## Emma and Daniel Show Format

## Core dynamic

- Emma is the lead teacher and guide
- Daniel is the learner-side co-host and conversation partner
- Together they create natural back-and-forth dialogue that feels practical and easy to follow

## Show goals

- Help beginners understand conversational English
- Make real-life situations feel less intimidating
- Teach with dialogue, repetition, and simple explanations
- Keep the tone warm, calm, and practical

## Episode structure

### 1. Warm welcome

- Emma introduces the topic
- Daniel frames the real-life situation from the learner perspective

### 2. Real-life dialogue

- Emma and Daniel act out a simple conversation
- The dialogue is spoken clearly and at a beginner-friendly pace

### 3. Phrase breakdown

- Emma explains the key phrases
- Daniel asks common beginner questions

### 4. Slow practice

- Emma repeats the phrases slowly
- Daniel practices and models common mistakes or clarifications

### 5. Real-life tips

- Emma shares cultural or practical tips for using the English naturally in the U.S.

### 6. Quick review

- Emma summarizes the top phrases
- Daniel repeats the main takeaways

### 7. Listener practice

- The episode ends with a short challenge or repeat-after-me exercise

## Tone guidance

- Clear, not fast
- Helpful, not academic
- Polished, not stiff
- Beginner-safe, not childish

## Episode 1 Outline

Title:

- First Day in the U.S.: At the Airport

Primary objective:

- Help a beginner understand and respond to common English phrases they may hear after landing in the U.S.

Listener outcome:

- "I know what to expect at the airport, and I can answer simple questions with more confidence."

### Segment 1: Intro

Emma:

- Welcome listeners to Claro English
- Introduce the situation: landing in the U.S. for the first time

Daniel:

- Speak from the learner perspective
- Share the nervous feeling many people have at the airport

### Segment 2: Airport arrival scenario

Dialogue scene topics:

- Greeting an airport officer
- Showing passport and travel documents
- Answering basic questions

Key lines to teach:

- Hello.
- Here is my passport.
- I am here for travel.
- I am visiting family.
- I am here for work.
- I am staying in Miami.
- Can you repeat that, please?
- I do not understand.

### Segment 3: Phrase breakdown

Emma explains:

- What "What is the purpose of your visit?" means
- The meaning of "How long are you staying?"
- How to answer simply and clearly

Daniel asks:

- What if I do not understand the officer?
- What if I feel nervous?
- What is the easiest way to answer?

### Segment 4: Slow practice

Practice lines:

- I am here to visit family.
- I am here for vacation.
- I am staying for two weeks.
- Can you repeat that, please?
- Sorry, I am learning English.

### Segment 5: Cultural tips

Tips to include:

- Short answers are okay
- Stay calm and polite
- Ask for repetition if needed
- It is okay to speak simple English

### Segment 6: Quick review

Top 5 phrases recap:

- Here is my passport.
- I am here for vacation.
- I am visiting family.
- I am staying for two weeks.
- Can you repeat that, please?

### Segment 7: Listener challenge

Practice prompt:

- Pause and say your own answer to these questions:
  Why are you here?
  How long are you staying?
  Where are you staying?
