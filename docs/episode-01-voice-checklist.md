# Episode 1 Voice Generation and Recording Checklist

## Goal

This checklist helps produce Emma, Daniel, and the officer voice consistently for Episode 1 and future Claro English episodes.

## Core Voice Principles

- Clear before expressive
- Warm before dramatic
- Beginner-friendly before fast
- Consistent pacing across the episode
- Natural and human-sounding, not robotic

## Voice Roles

### Emma

Role:
- Lead teacher and guide

Voice qualities:
- Calm
- Warm
- Clear
- Supportive
- Confident

Delivery rules:
- Speak at a moderate to slow pace
- Slow down more during teaching and repetition sections
- Avoid sounding too formal or too excited
- Keep pronunciation crisp and easy to understand

### Daniel

Role:
- Learner-side co-host and conversation partner

Voice qualities:
- Friendly
- Natural
- Curious
- Supportive
- Slightly more conversational than Emma

Delivery rules:
- Sound like a helpful learner, not a confused character
- Ask questions naturally
- Reflect listener nervousness without sounding anxious the whole time
- Keep the tone reassuring and relaxed

### Officer

Role:
- Airport officer in roleplay scenes

Voice qualities:
- Neutral
- Clear
- Brief
- Slightly firmer than Emma

Delivery rules:
- Keep lines short and direct
- Do not sound aggressive
- Do not overact authority

## Recording / TTS Setup Checklist

### For Emma

- Use a neutral American English accent
- Set speaking speed slightly slower than default
- Prioritize clarity over personality range
- Use consistent energy across the entire episode
- Test phrase repetition sections separately before final render

### For Daniel

- Use a neutral American English accent
- Set speaking speed at normal or slightly slow
- Keep emotional tone gentle and conversational
- Make sure Daniel does not sound too similar to Emma

### For Officer

- Use the same voice actor or TTS family only if clearly distinguishable
- Slightly reduce warmth and increase firmness
- Keep speed steady and easy to follow

## Pre-Generation Checklist

Before generating final audio, confirm:

- Pronunciation of "Claro English"
- Pronunciation of "Miami"
- Consistent use of "U.S." or "United States"
- Correct pacing in repeat-after-me lines
- Natural pause spacing after questions

## Line-Level Performance Notes

### Emma key moments

Focus lines:
- Welcome to Claro English, where you learn real English for real life in the U.S.
- Your English does not need to be perfect. It just needs to be clear.
- You do not need perfect English. You need clear English you can really use.

Performance note:
- These are signature lines and should sound especially warm, grounded, and memorable.

### Daniel key moments

Focus lines:
- That feeling is very real.
- This part is important.
- Great job.

Performance note:
- Daniel should make the listener feel seen and supported.

### Officer key moments

Focus lines:
- Passport, please.
- What is the purpose of your visit?
- How long are you staying?
- Where are you staying?

Performance note:
- These must be extra clear because learners are training their ears on them.

## Recommended Recording Workflow

1. Generate or record Emma's full track
2. Generate or record Daniel's full track
3. Generate the officer lines separately
4. Review all repeated teaching phrases for pacing consistency
5. Adjust pauses before exporting the final mix

## Pronunciation Watchlist

- passport
- purpose
- visit
- vacation
- family
- hotel
- Miami
- repeat
- learning English

## Pause Guidance

Use longer pauses than normal content because learners need processing time.

Recommended pause ranges:

- Between short teaching lines: 0.5 to 0.75 seconds
- After repeat-after-me prompts: 1.25 to 1.75 seconds
- During listener practice questions: 2 to 3 seconds

## Audio Quality Checklist

- No harsh sibilance
- No overly synthetic intonation
- No abrupt volume shifts between speakers
- Emma and Daniel levels are balanced
- Officer lines are clear but not louder than the hosts
- Background music never competes with the voices

## Final QA Checklist

Before publishing, verify:

- Emma sounds consistent from start to finish
- Daniel feels distinct from Emma
- The officer voice is understandable on first listen
- Repeat-after-me sections leave enough time for the listener
- The emotional tone is calm and confidence-building
- Hard words like "purpose" and "vacation" are clearly pronounced
