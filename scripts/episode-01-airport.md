# Episode 1 Script

## Series

Claro English

## Episode Title

First Day in the U.S.: At the Airport

## Primary Tagline

Real English for Real Life in the U.S.

## Supporting Goal

Help beginner Spanish-speaking listeners understand and respond to simple English questions they may hear at the airport after arriving in the United States.

## Listener Outcome

By the end of this episode, the listener should feel more confident hearing common airport questions and giving short, clear answers in English.

## Cast

- Emma, lead host and teacher
- Daniel, co-host and learner-side conversation partner

## Tone Notes

- Warm
- Calm
- Clear
- Beginner-friendly
- Practical, not academic

## Full Script

### Opening

Emma:
Welcome to Claro English, where you learn real English for real life in the U.S.

Emma:
I'm Emma.

Daniel:
And I'm Daniel.

Emma:
If you are a beginner and you want to understand everyday English more clearly, you are in the right place.

Daniel:
In this episode, we're talking about something many people feel nervous about: arriving at the airport in the United States.

Emma:
Yes. Maybe it is your first time in the U.S. Maybe you are tired. Maybe you are worried because you do not know what the immigration officer will ask.

Daniel:
That feeling is very real. You want to understand the questions. You want to answer correctly. And you do not want to panic.

Emma:
Exactly. So today, we will help you with simple English you can use at the airport. We will practice important questions, easy answers, and polite phrases you can use if you do not understand.

Daniel:
And remember: your English does not need to be perfect. It just needs to be clear.

Emma:
That is right. Let's begin.

### Segment 1: The Situation

Emma:
Imagine this. You land in the United States. You get off the plane. You follow the signs. Now you are standing in line at passport control.

Daniel:
You have your passport. Maybe you have travel documents. Maybe you are a little nervous.

Emma:
Then the officer looks at you and asks a question in English.

Daniel:
Let's hear a simple example.

### Segment 2: First Dialogue

Officer, voiced by Emma:
Hello.

Traveler, voiced by Daniel:
Hello.

Officer:
Passport, please.

Traveler:
Here is my passport.

Officer:
What is the purpose of your visit?

Traveler:
I am here for vacation.

Officer:
How long are you staying?

Traveler:
I am staying for two weeks.

Officer:
Where are you staying?

Traveler:
I am staying at a hotel in Miami.

Officer:
Thank you.

Traveler:
Thank you.

### Segment 3: First Breakdown

Emma:
Good. Now let's slow that down.

Daniel:
Yes, because when you hear it quickly, it can feel difficult.

Emma:
Passport, please.

Emma:
This is simple. The officer wants to see your passport.

Daniel:
A good answer is:

Daniel:
Here is my passport.

Emma:
Let's say that together.

Emma and Daniel:
Here is my passport.

Emma:
One more time.

Emma and Daniel:
Here is my passport.

Emma:
Next question:

Emma:
What is the purpose of your visit?

This sounds long.

Emma:
Yes, but the meaning is simple. The officer is asking why you came to the U.S.

Daniel:
Maybe for vacation. Maybe for work. Maybe to visit family.

Emma:
Exactly. Here are three simple answers.

Emma:
I am here for vacation.

Daniel:
I am here for work.

Emma:
I am visiting family.

Emma:
Let's repeat them slowly.

Emma and Daniel:
I am here for vacation.

Emma and Daniel:
I am here for work.

Emma and Daniel:
I am visiting family.

### Segment 4: Common Airport Question

Emma:
Now let's look at the next question.

Emma:
How long are you staying?

Emma:

Emma:

Daniel:
I am staying for two weeks.

Emma and Daniel:

Another common question is:

Emma:
Where are you staying?

Emma:
Simple answers can sound like this.

Emma:
I am staying at a hotel in Miami.

Daniel:
I am staying with my family in Miami.

Emma:
I am staying at a hotel.

Emma:
Repeat with us.

Emma and Daniel:
I am staying at a hotel in Miami.

Emma and Daniel:
I am staying with my family in Miami.

Emma and Daniel:
I am staying at a hotel.

### Segment 5: What If You Do Not Understand?

This part is important. What if the officer speaks fast and I do not understand?

Emma:

Emma:

Emma:

Daniel:

Emma:
Sorry, I am learning English.

Daniel:

Daniel:

Emma and Daniel:

Emma and Daniel:
Sorry, I am learning English.

Emma:
One more time, slowly.

Emma and Daniel:

Emma and Daniel:
Sorry, I am learning English.

### Segment 7: Real-Life Tips

Emma:
Here are a few real-life tips for the airport.

Emma:
Tip one: short answers are okay.

Daniel:
You do not need long, complicated English.

Emma:
Tip two: speak clearly and calmly.

Daniel:
Even simple English is good when it is clear.

Emma:
Tip three: if you do not understand, ask the person to repeat.

Daniel:
That is better than guessing.

Emma:
Tip four: it is okay to say you are learning English.

Daniel:
That simple sentence can help a lot.
