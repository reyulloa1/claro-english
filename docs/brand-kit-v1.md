# Claro English Brand Kit v1

## Purpose

This brand kit defines the visual system for Claro English across website, YouTube, podcast artwork, social graphics, thumbnails, and future app surfaces.

## Brand Positioning Reminder

Claro English helps Spanish speakers learn real English for real life in the U.S.

The visual identity should feel:

- Clear
- Warm
- Modern
- Encouraging
- Professional without feeling corporate

## Core Tagline System

Primary tagline:

- Real English for Real Life in the U.S.

Supporting taglines:

- Your First English for Life in the U.S.
- Clear English for Your New Life in America

Usage rule:
Use the primary tagline on major brand surfaces. Use the supporting taglines sparingly on campaign graphics, subpages, or secondary social visuals.

## Logo Direction

### Recommended logo style

- Wordmark-first brand identity
- Clean, editorial feel
- Friendly but polished character
- Works on light and warm-toned backgrounds

### Recommended treatment

- Main wordmark: Claro English
- Secondary lockup: Claro English + primary tagline

## Color System

### Primary colors

- Warm Cream: #F7F1E8
- Soft Sand: #F0E1CC
- Deep Ink: #1F2430
- Claro Orange: #D65C3A
- Claro Rust: #AA3D22

### Supporting colors

- Accent Blush: #F4CDBD
- Surface White: #FFFAF2
- Muted Slate: #5B6470

### Usage guidance

- Cream and sand should carry most backgrounds
- Deep ink should carry most text
- Claro orange should be used for CTAs, highlights, and important phrase accents
- Rust should be used for emphasis, eyebrow labels, and deeper visual anchors

## Typography Direction

### Website and brand headlines

Recommended style:
- Strong editorial serif
- Warm and trustworthy, not stiff

Current foundation:
- Georgia, Times New Roman, serif fallback

### Body text and UI text

Recommended style:
- Clean humanist sans-serif
- Easy to read on web and video graphics

Current foundation:
- Trebuchet MS, Gill Sans, sans-serif fallback

### Usage guidance

- Use serif for headlines and major titles
- Use sans-serif for body copy, captions, buttons, metadata, and overlays
- Keep phrase cards large and readable

## Host Visual System

### Emma

Visual direction:
- Calm, warm, polished
- Approachable facial expression
- Modern styling
- Clear lighting and gentle contrast

### Daniel

Visual direction:
- Friendly, grounded, supportive
- Slightly more conversational energy than Emma
- Consistent styling family with Emma

### Shared rules

- Both hosts should feel like part of the same brand world
- Avoid hyperreal uncanny renders
- Avoid overly futuristic AI visuals
- Favor premium-friendly portraits, illustrations, or lightly stylized digital hosts

## Platform Visual Rules

### Website

- Warm editorial layout
- Spacious sections
- Rounded cards
- Phrase chips and section cards in the same style as video overlays

### YouTube full episodes

- Clean intro card
- Large readable phrase overlays
- Consistent lower-thirds for Emma and Daniel
- Calm b-roll and simple motion graphics

### YouTube thumbnails

- Emma and Daniel visible when possible
- Short bold text only
- Warm airport or real-life background
- Orange highlight for urgency or focus
- Keep clutter low

### Podcast cover art

- Simple and bold
- Claro English name readable at small sizes
- Optional Emma and Daniel visual pairing
- Use the same warm cream, deep ink, and orange system

### Instagram

- Carousel cards should reuse phrase card style
- Reels should reuse subtitle style and section colors
- Graphics should feel like a cropped extension of the website and YouTube visuals

### X

- Text-first, but image posts should still use phrase card templates or quote cards from the core brand system

## Reusable Graphic Components

Create these reusable templates first:

- Logo card
- Intro title card
- End card
- Host lower-third
- Section card
- Phrase card
- Quote card
- Tip card
- Thumbnail template
- Carousel slide template
- Social story template

## Layout Characteristics

- Rounded corners
- Soft gradients
- Generous white space
- Large typography for teaching moments
- One idea per frame during lesson sections

## Motion Guidance

- Keep animation subtle
- Use fades, slides, and soft reveals
- Avoid aggressive zooms or flashy transitions
- Motion should help comprehension, not distract from it

## Photography / Illustration Direction

Preferred:
- Bright airport and everyday-life scenes
- Friendly real-world environments
- Calm, optimistic framing

Avoid:
- Crowded stressful scenes
- Dark moody visuals
- Generic tech imagery
- Neon AI imagery that feels detached from everyday life

## Brand Voice in Visual Copy

Visual text should sound:

- Practical
- Calm
- Clear
- Supportive

Avoid copy that feels:

- Too academic
- Too salesy
- Too slang-heavy
- Too childish

## Episode-Level Consistency Rules

For every episode, keep these consistent:

- Host naming and lower-thirds
- Section card style
- Phrase card style
- Subtitle style
- CTA placement
- End card format
- Color hierarchy

## Production Starter Pack Recommendation

The first assets to actually design should be:

1. Logo wordmark
2. Website visual theme matching the current site foundation
3. Emma and Daniel host portraits
4. YouTube thumbnail template
5. Phrase card template
6. Intro and end cards
7. Instagram carousel template

## Alignment With Current Website Foundation

The website foundation in `/site` already uses this core visual system:

- Warm cream backgrounds
- Deep ink text
- Claro orange accents
- Editorial headline style
- Rounded cards and soft gradients

That means the site can now act as the visual reference point for the broader brand system.
