# Brand Package v1

## Overview

This document turns the brand strategy into concrete options for review:

- Brand name direction
- AI host and co-host persona
- Tagline options
- First 10 episode concepts

## Naming Principles

The name should feel:

- Easy to say
- Trustworthy
- Welcoming to Spanish speakers
- Relevant to English learning and real life in the U.S.
- Broad enough to support a website, app, podcast, and premium product

Recommendation:
Avoid names that are too childish, too academic, or too hard to spell.

## Final Brand Name

### Claro English

Why it works:

- "Claro" is familiar and positive for Spanish speakers
- Suggests clarity and confidence
- Short and memorable
- Works well as a media brand and app name
- Feels simple enough for beginners and polished enough to grow premium later

Brand feel:
Clear, modern, supportive

Confirmed direction:

- Brand name: Claro English

## AI Host Persona

### Primary host: Emma

Why it works:

- Easy to pronounce in English and Spanish
- Friendly and international
- Fits a calm and modern host identity
- Strong fit for a teaching-first brand

Suggested personality:

- Warm
- Encouraging
- Clear
- Patient
- Organized

Suggested host intro style:

"Hi, I'm Emma, and I'm here to help you understand real English for everyday life in the U.S."

## Co-Host Recommendation

Because the show will benefit from back-and-forth dialogue, a male co-host is a strong addition.

### Recommended male co-host: Daniel

Why it works:

- Easy to pronounce in English and Spanish
- Warm and trustworthy
- Feels natural next to Emma
- Strong fit for dialogue-driven teaching

Suggested role:

- Emma explains and guides
- Daniel plays the learner, asks questions, practices phrases, and helps surface common beginner confusion

Alternative male co-host options:

- Alex
- Leo
- David

Best overall pairing:

- Emma and Daniel

## Visual Direction For The Hosts

Recommendation:
Use a clean, premium-friendly portrait illustration or polished AI-generated host image with:

- Warm expression
- Soft modern colors
- Professional but approachable styling
- Consistent framing across YouTube, website, and app

Avoid:

- Overly futuristic sci-fi styling
- Uncanny hyperreal avatars
- Cartoonish mascot design

## Show Format Recommendation

The early episode slate should be tied together by a story framework that follows a learner through their first day, first week, and first key moments in the U.S.

This is a strong idea because it makes the content:

- More emotionally relatable
- Easier to sequence
- Easier to binge
- Easier to turn into a podcast series or app path

Recommended framing:

- Season 1: Your First Days in the U.S.

## First 10 Episode Concepts

These are designed for beginners and should be highly practical, searchable, and emotionally useful.

### Episode 1

First Day in the U.S.: At the Airport

Focus:

- Passport and immigration basics
- Understanding airport questions
- Asking for help
- Simple arrival phrases

### Episode 2

After the Airport: Getting a Ride and Going to Your Hotel or Home

Focus:

- Transportation vocabulary
- Confirming destinations
- Understanding simple directions
- Common ride-share or taxi phrases

### Episode 3

Checking In: Meeting New People and Introducing Yourself

Focus:

- Name
- Where you are from
- What you do
- Simple small talk starters

### Episode 4

Your First Meal: Ordering Food in English

Focus:

- Restaurant phrases
- Fast food phrases
- Common questions
- How to ask for changes politely

### Episode 5

Getting Around: Asking for Directions and Understanding the Answer

Focus:

- Places in a city
- Direction vocabulary
- Clarifying when you do not understand

### Episode 6

Shopping for Essentials: Prices, Sizes, and Paying

Focus:

- Asking where things are
- Understanding sizes and prices
- Paying at checkout
- Returning an item

### Episode 7

Your First Day at Work: Talking to Coworkers

Focus:

- Greetings
- Asking for help
- Understanding instructions
- Workplace politeness

### Episode 8

Making Small Talk in Simple English

Focus:

- Friendly questions
- Weather and daily life conversation
- How to continue a conversation naturally

### Episode 9

What to Say on the Phone in English

Focus:

- Answering calls
- Leaving messages
- Asking someone to repeat
- Common phone phrases

### Episode 10

What to Say at a Doctor Appointment in English

Focus:

- Describing symptoms
- Basic questions
- Insurance and appointment words
- Emergency vs non-emergency language

## Season 2 Direction

After the first-day or first-week storyline, the next set of episodes can move into:

- Renting an apartment in the U.S.
- Talking to a school or teacher
- Going to the bank
- Workplace English
- Emergency situations
- Making friends and building confidence
